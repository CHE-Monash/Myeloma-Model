# Licensing & open science

The **Monash Myeloma Model** follows **open-science principles** — the full source code and model
documentation are publicly available for inspection and reproduction, and the methods are described in
peer-reviewed publications — under a **dual, source-available licence**. We follow the principle
*"as open as possible, as closed as necessary."*

> **Not "open source" in the OSI sense.** Because commercial use is restricted, this project is
> **source-available**, not open source. Please describe it that way.

## The two licences

| Use | Licence | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Noncommercial** — academic, educational, public-research, government, personal, and reproduction of published analyses | [PolyForm Noncommercial License 1.0.0](LICENSE) | Free |
| **Commercial** — any commercial purpose, including use in or preparation of **industry-sponsored regulatory or reimbursement (e.g. PBAC) submissions** | Separate commercial licence from Monash University | By negotiation |

Under the PolyForm Noncommercial License, universities, hospitals, public research organisations and
government bodies may use, modify and build on the model **regardless of their funding source** — so
academic reproducibility and collaboration are fully supported.

Two common industry paths:

1. **Consultancy** — Monash runs the analysis and delivers the results. You are buying an output, not
   the software, so no software licence is required (the engagement is governed by a Monash consultancy
   agreement and the MRDR data terms).
2. **Commercial licence** — you run the model yourself, under a negotiated commercial licence.

## Data and fitted parameters

The **fitted model parameters** (coefficient sets) and the **patient cohorts included in this repository**
are provided under the same licence as the code, so the **published analyses can be reproduced directly
from the repository** — no separate data request is required.

The **underlying individual-patient MRDR registry data** from which the model is estimated is **not
included here**: it is restricted and governed by the **MRDR data agreement** (Australia and New Zealand
Myeloma and Related Diseases Registry), with access via the MRDR Steering Committee —
[mrdr.net.au](https://www.mrdr.net.au/). That raw registry data is needed only to **re-estimate the model
or build new patient populations** — not to reproduce the published results.
